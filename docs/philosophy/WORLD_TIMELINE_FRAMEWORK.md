## 世界时间线框架

---

# 2. The Meaning of Time in The Wayfarer

In The Wayfarer, time is not merely a background setting.

Time is the fundamental structure through which the world exists and evolves.

Everything exists within time, including:

- Life;
- Individuals;
- Ideas;
- Societies;
- Civilizations.

Time does not only record changes.

Time carries the process of change itself.

The world continues to evolve regardless of player actions.

Even when players do not interact with the world:

- Nature continues changing;
- Life continues evolving;
- Society continues developing;
- History continues moving forward.

---

# 3. Principle of Continuous Evolution

The Wayfarer does not intend to represent history as a collection of isolated events.

In reality, major events are usually not created instantly.

They often emerge from:

- Countless small decisions;
- Gradual environmental changes;
- Social interactions;
- Long-term accumulation.

Therefore, The Wayfarer focuses not only on:

**What happened?**

but also:

**How did it happen?**

---

The current design direction does not prioritize free time jumping.

The project emphasizes:

- Experiencing processes;
- Understanding development;
- Observing accumulation;
- Reflecting on consequences.

The value of historical exploration comes from experiencing the path that leads to an outcome.

---

# 4. Earth Timeline Structure

The Wayfarer uses the real development history of Earth as a primary reference.

The world evolves continuously through:

Past → Present → Future


Time does not reset because of player existence.

Players enter an already developing world.

They become part of the timeline rather than the center of the timeline.

---

# 5. Preliminary Earth Evolution Stages

The following stages represent the current high-level framework.

They are not a complete historical classification.

Future versions may refine these structures.

---

## 5.1 Earth Formation Stage

Explores:

- Formation of Earth;
- Geological development;
- Formation of oceans;
- Atmospheric changes.

This stage establishes the environmental foundation before life appears.

---

## 5.2 Life Evolution Stage

Explores:

- Origin of life;
- Single-cell organisms;
- Multicellular organisms;
- Biological evolution;
- Ecosystem development.

During this stage, the player's relationship with the world may differ from the human stage.

Possible directions include:

- Observer perspective;
- Environmental exploration;
- Limited interaction.

Specific gameplay mechanisms have not yet been designed.

---

## 5.3 Human Emergence Stage

Explores:

- Human origins;
- Tool development;
- Language;
- Early social structures.

The player's role may gradually transition from observer to participant.

---

## 5.4 Civilization Development Stage

This is one of the core exploration stages of The Wayfarer.

Explores:

- Formation of civilizations;
- Social organization;
- Development of states;
- Technology advancement;
- Cultural evolution.

The focus is not only showing civilization achievements.

The focus is understanding:

**How civilizations are formed.**

---

## 5.5 Modern Civilization Stage

Explores:

- Industrial development;
- Information age;
- Globalization;
- Technological transformation.

This stage focuses on interactions between:

- Individuals;
- Societies;
- Nations;
- International systems;
- Civilization development.

---

## 5.6 Future Exploration Stage

The future is not considered a fixed answer.

Future development may be influenced by:

- Technology;
- Environment;
- Social choices;
- Individual decisions;
- Civilization direction.

The Wayfarer does not attempt to predict a single future.

It explores possible futures.

---

# 6. Event Evolution Through Time

The Wayfarer does not treat events as isolated points.

Events are processes.

They contain:

- Origins;
- Development;
- Accumulation;
- Transformation;
- Consequences.

A major event may be influenced by multiple dimensions:

- Personal dimension;
- Social dimension;
- National dimension;
- International dimension;
- Civilization dimension.

These influences interact and accumulate through time.

---

# 7. Causal Exploration

The Wayfarer encourages players to explore:

**Why?**

not only:

**What happened?**

Players should be able to understand:

- Which choices influenced events;
- Which factors accumulated over time;
- How small changes created large consequences.

The purpose is not only remembering history.

The purpose is understanding the mechanisms behind history.

---

# 8. Player and Time

Players are not outside of time.

Players are part of the timeline.

Every choice may:

- Create influence;
- Leave records;
- Affect future possibilities.

The player's actions may become part of the world's ongoing evolution.

---
