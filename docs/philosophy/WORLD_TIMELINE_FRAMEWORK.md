# 2. The Meaning of Time in The Wayfarer

In The Wayfarer, time is not merely a background setting.

Time is the fundamental structure through which the world exists and evolves.

Everything exists within time, including:

- Life;
- Individuals;
- Ideas;
- Societies;
- Civilizations.

Time does not only record changes.

Time carries the process of change itself.

The world continues to evolve regardless of player actions.

Even when players do not interact with the world:

- Nature continues changing;
- Life continues evolving;
- Society continues developing;
- History continues moving forward.

Players enter a world that already exists within an ongoing timeline.

------

# 3. Principle of Continuous Evolution

The Wayfarer does not represent history as a collection of isolated events.

Major changes are usually not created instantly.

They emerge through:

- countless small decisions;
- environmental changes;
- social interactions;
- technological development;
- cultural evolution;
- long-term accumulation.

Therefore, The Wayfarer focuses not only on:

**What happened?**

but also:

**How did it happen?**

The value of historical exploration comes from experiencing the process that creates outcomes.

------

# 4. Earth Timeline Structure

The Wayfarer uses the real development history of Earth as a primary reference.

The world evolves continuously through:

Past → Present → Future

Time does not reset because of player existence.

Players become participants within history rather than the center of history.

The timeline provides the foundation upon which all other systems develop.

------

# 5. Preliminary Earth Evolution Stages

The following stages represent the current high-level framework.

They are not a complete historical classification.

Future versions may refine these structures.

------

# 5.1 Earth Formation Stage

Explores:

- Formation of Earth;
- Geological development;
- Formation of oceans;
- Atmospheric changes;
- Environmental foundations.

This stage establishes the conditions required for future life development.

------

# 5.2 Life Evolution Stage

Explores:

- Origin of life;
- Single-cell organisms;
- Multicellular organisms;
- Biological evolution;
- Ecosystem development.

During this stage, the player's relationship with the world differs from the human stage.

Possible directions include:

- Observer perspective;
- Environmental exploration;
- Limited interaction.

Specific gameplay mechanisms remain future design work.

------

# 5.3 Human Emergence Stage

Explores:

- Human origins;
- Tool development;
- Language;
- Early social structures;
- Human cooperation.

During this stage, the player's role may gradually transition from observer to participant.

------

# 5.4 Civilization Development Stage

This is one of the core exploration stages of The Wayfarer.

Explores:

- Formation of civilizations;
- Social organization;
- Development of states;
- Technology advancement;
- Cultural evolution.

The focus is not only showing civilization achievements.

The focus is understanding:

**How civilizations are formed.**

------

# 5.5 Modern Civilization Stage

Explores:

- Industrial development;
- Information age;
- Globalization;
- Technological transformation.

This stage focuses on interactions between:

- Individuals;
- Communities;
- Nations;
- International systems;
- Global civilization.

------

# 5.6 Future Exploration Stage

The future is not considered a fixed answer.

The future is a space of possibilities created through:

- Technology;
- Environment;
- Social choices;
- Individual decisions;
- Cultural development;
- Civilization direction.

The Wayfarer does not attempt to predict a single future.

It explores possible futures.

------

# 6. Multi-Dimensional Evolution Within Time

Time is the fundamental dimension of The Wayfarer.

Within the timeline, multiple dimensions develop independently while continuously interacting.

These dimensions include:

- Life dimension;
- Individual dimension;
- Social dimension;
- Cultural dimension;
- Technological dimension;
- Economic dimension;
- Political dimension;
- Civilization dimension;
- Trust and Contract dimension.

Each dimension follows its own development process.

However, these dimensions may intersect and influence each other through time.

The world emerges from the interaction between multiple evolving dimensions.

------

# 7. Trust and Contract Dimension

Trust is a special dimension within The Wayfarer.

Unlike temporary resources, trust accumulates through time.

Individual actions, social behaviors, and historical decisions create trust records.

Trust influences:

- Relationships;
- Cooperation;
- Trade;
- Diplomacy;
- Social stability;
- Civilization development.

When individuals or societies interact, previous behavior becomes part of future decision-making.

Trust becomes a form of historical memory carried through time.

A person's or civilization's reputation is not created by a single moment.

It is accumulated through continuous actions.

------

# 8. Event Evolution Through Time

The Wayfarer does not treat events as isolated points.

Events are processes.

They contain:

- Origins;
- Development;
- Accumulation;
- Transformation;
- Consequences.

A major event may be influenced by multiple dimensions:

- Personal dimension;
- Social dimension;
- National dimension;
- International dimension;
- Civilization dimension.

These influences interact and accumulate through time.

------

# 9. Individual Influence and Historical Accumulation

The Wayfarer recognizes that large-scale historical changes may begin with small individual actions.

An individual decision may influence:

Individual

↓

Family

↓

Community

↓

Region

↓

Nation

↓

Global Systems

↓

Civilization Development

Through time, small influences may accumulate into significant changes.

The project explores how individual choices become part of larger historical processes.

------

# 10. Causal Exploration

The Wayfarer encourages players to explore:

**Why?**

not only:

**What happened?**

Players should be able to understand:

- Which choices influenced events;
- Which factors accumulated over time;
- How small changes created large consequences;
- How different dimensions interacted.

The purpose is not only remembering history.

The purpose is understanding the mechanisms behind history.

------

# 11. Player and Time

Players are not outside of time.

Players are part of the timeline.

Depending on the stage of world development, players may experience different roles.

## Observer

In early stages of world development:

Players may observe natural processes and environmental changes.

They may influence conditions without directly controlling outcomes.

------

## Participant

As life and societies develop:

Players become individuals within the world.

They experience:

- Relationships;
- Culture;
- Society;
- Personal development.

------

## Decision Maker

At advanced civilization stages:

Players may influence larger systems through:

- Leadership;
- Cooperation;
- Innovation;
- Decision-making.

Players do not completely control history.

They become one of the factors that shape history.
