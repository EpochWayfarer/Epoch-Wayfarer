# The Wayfarer Contributor Guidelines

## Version 1.0

---

# Introduction

The Wayfarer is an open exploration project built through the cooperation of people from different fields, cultures, and generations.

The project believes that understanding humanity requires contributions from many perspectives.

Science, technology, education, law, economics, culture, creativity, and human experience are all valuable parts of this exploration.

The Wayfarer welcomes contributors who share the project's vision and respect its founding principles.

---

# Core Principle

Every contributor is first a learner.

No individual can fully understand every field of human knowledge.

A scientist may learn from educators.

A programmer may learn from historians.

A designer may learn from psychologists.

An expert in one field may become a learner in another.

The Wayfarer encourages humility, curiosity, cooperation, and continuous learning.

---

# What Is a Contributor?

A contributor is anyone who helps improve The Wayfarer through:

- knowledge;
- research;
- education;
- technology;
- creativity;
- experience;
- feedback;
- discussion.

Contribution is not limited to writing code.

Every meaningful contribution that supports the project's mission is valuable.

---

# Contribution Process

The Wayfarer encourages a structured contribution process.

A general workflow:

```
Idea or Contribution

↓

Discussion

↓

Review

↓

Integration

↓

Implementation

↓

Evaluation

↓

Improvement
```

Different contributions may require different review methods.

---

# Contribution Principles

All contributors should respect the following principles:

## 1. Respect the Founding Principles

The project's core vision and fundamental principles should be preserved.

Contributions should strengthen the project rather than change its original purpose.

---

## 2. Respect Different Disciplines

No single discipline represents the complete truth.

The Wayfarer values cooperation between different fields.

---

## 3. Focus on Long-Term Value

The project prioritizes meaningful and sustainable development.

Short-term popularity should not replace long-term purpose.

---

## 4. Encourage Open Discussion

Different opinions are valuable.

Constructive discussion helps improve ideas.

---

## 5. Maintain Responsibility

Contributors should consider:

- accuracy;
- ethics;
- legal responsibility;
- social impact.

---

# Intellectual Property and Contributions

Contributors should clearly understand the relationship between contribution and project development.

Before making significant contributions, contributors should follow the project's official contribution agreements and policies.

These policies exist to:

- protect contributors;
- protect the project;
- ensure long-term sustainability;
- avoid future legal conflicts.

Specific legal agreements may evolve as the project develops.

---

# Respect for Original Vision

The Wayfarer welcomes innovation and new ideas.

However, participation does not mean changing the project's fundamental direction.

The project encourages exploration within its established principles.

The purpose of collaboration is to expand understanding, not replace the original mission.

---

# Global Participation

The Wayfarer welcomes contributors from:

- different countries;
- different cultures;
- different academic backgrounds;
- different generations.

Human knowledge belongs to everyone.

The project hopes to create a place where people can cooperate across traditional boundaries.

---

# Final Statement

The Wayfarer is built by many people, but guided by a shared purpose:

To explore human knowledge, cognition, creativity, and civilization through meaningful interactive experiences.

Every contributor adds a piece to this exploration.

Every contribution represents another step in understanding ourselves and the world around us.
